Categorize: Does the object have kinetic energy?

1. A rolling ball

YES - The ball is moving, so it has KE

2. A stationary car in a parking lot

NO - The car is not moving, so KE = 0

3. A flying airplane

YES - The airplane is moving through the air

4. A book resting on a table

NO - The book is stationary

5. A spinning top

YES - The top is rotating/moving

6. A falling raindrop

YES - The raindrop is falling (moving downward)

Categorize by KE comparison:

Which object has MORE kinetic energy?

1. A 2kg object at 10 m/s OR a 10kg object at 2 m/s?

Object 1: KE = ½ × 2 × (10)² = 1 × 100 = 100 J
Object 2: KE = ½ × 10 × (2)² = 5 × 4 = 20 J
The 2kg object at 10 m/s has MORE KE

2. A car at 20 m/s OR a car at 10 m/s (same mass)?

The car at 20 m/s has MORE KE (4 times more because velocity is squared)

3. A bicycle moving at 5 m/s OR a truck at 2 m/s?

Depends on mass. Generally truck has more KE due to much larger mass, despite slower speed

4. An object moving forward at 10 m/s OR backward at 10 m/s?

SAME KE - KE depends on speed (magnitude), not direction. Both have same KE

5. A heavy slow object OR a light fast object?

The light fast object - velocity is squared in KE formula, so speed matters more than mass

6. An object at rest OR any moving object?

The moving object has MORE KE (stationary has KE = 0)
