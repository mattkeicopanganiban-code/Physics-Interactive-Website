Question 1: Gravitational PE = m × ______ × h

Answer: g (gravity or gravitational acceleration). The formula is PE = mgh

Question 2: If m = 4kg, g = 10 m/s², h = 5m, then PE = ______ Joules

Answer: 200. PE = 4 × 10 × 5 = 200 Joules

Question 3: Rearrange to find height: h = PE ÷ (m × ______)

Answer: g. From PE = mgh, we get h = PE ÷ (mg)

Question 4: For elastic PE, if k = 200 N/m and x = 0.5m, then PE = ______ Joules

Answer: 25. PE = ½ × 200 × (0.5)² = 100 × 0.25 = 25 Joules

Question 5: An object with PE = 500J at height 10m, find mass (g = 10 m/s²): m = ______ kg

Answer: 5. m = PE ÷ (g × h) = 500 ÷ (10 × 10) = 5 kg

Question 6: A 6kg object is lifted from 2m to 8m height. Change in PE = ______ Joules (g = 10)

Answer: 360. ΔPE = PE₂ - PE₁ = (6×10×8) - (6×10×2) = 480 - 120 = 360 Joules
