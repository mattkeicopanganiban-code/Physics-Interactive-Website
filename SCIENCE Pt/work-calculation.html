Problem 1: Basic Work Calculation

A person pushes a box with 100N of force across the floor for 8 meters. Calculate the work done.

Work = Force × Distance = 100N × 8m = 800 Joules

Problem 2: Finding Distance

If 2000 Joules of work is done by a 50N force, how far was the object moved?

Distance = Work ÷ Force = 2000J ÷ 50N = 40 meters

Problem 3: Finding Force

A 5000 Joule of work is done to move an object 25 meters. What force was applied?

Force = Work ÷ Distance = 5000J ÷ 25m = 200 Newtons

Problem 4: Power Calculation

A worker does 1200 Joules of work in 4 seconds. What is the power output?

Power = Work ÷ Time = 1200J ÷ 4s = 300 Watts

Problem 5: Multi-step Problem

An elevator lifts a 600kg person 10 meters up in 5 seconds. Calculate (a) the work done and (b) the power output. (Use g = 10 m/s²)

(a) Work = Force × Distance = (mass × g) × height = (600 × 10) × 10 = 60,000 Joules
(b) Power = Work ÷ Time = 60,000J ÷ 5s = 12,000 Watts or 12 kilowatts

Problem 6: Work with Angle

A person pulls a sled with 150N at an angle of 0° (parallel to motion) for 20 meters. Calculate work done.

Work = F × d × cos(θ) = 150N × 20m × cos(0°) = 150 × 20 × 1 = 3000 Joules
(cos(0°) = 1, so full force contributes to work)
