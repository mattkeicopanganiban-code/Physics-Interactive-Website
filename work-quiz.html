Potential Energy Quiz - Test Your Knowledge!

Question 1: What is the formula for gravitational potential energy?

A) PE = mgh
B) PE = ½mv²
C) PE = Fd
D) PE = ma
PE = mgh, where m is mass, g is gravity (9.8 m/s² or 10 m/s²), and h is height above reference point

Question 2: A 5kg object is lifted to a height of 3 meters. What is its potential energy? (Use g = 10 m/s²)

A) 50 Joules
B) 100 Joules
C) 150 Joules
D) 200 Joules
PE = mgh = 5kg × 10 m/s² × 3m = 150 Joules

Question 3: What happens to potential energy when an object falls?

A) It increases
B) It decreases
C) It stays the same
D) It changes randomly
PE decreases as height decreases. The PE is converted to KE as the object falls

Question 4: Which type of potential energy does a stretched spring have?

A) Gravitational PE
B) Elastic PE
C) Chemical PE
D) Thermal PE
Elastic PE is energy stored in deformed elastic objects like springs, rubber bands, and bowstrings

Question 5: An object has 500 Joules of potential energy at a height of 10m. What is its mass? (Use g = 10 m/s²)

A) 3 kg
B) 4 kg
C) 5 kg
D) 6 kg
m = PE ÷ (g × h) = 500 ÷ (10 × 10) = 500 ÷ 100 = 5 kg
