Problem 1: Basic Kinetic Energy

A 3kg object moves at 6 m/s. Calculate its kinetic energy.

KE = ½mv² = ½ × 3 × (6)² = 1.5 × 36 = 54 Joules

Problem 2: Finding Velocity

An object has 200 Joules of kinetic energy and a mass of 4kg. What is its velocity?

v = √(2 × KE ÷ m) = √(2 × 200 ÷ 4) = √(400 ÷ 4) = √100 = 10 m/s

Problem 3: Finding Mass

An object has 150 Joules of kinetic energy and is moving at 10 m/s. What is its mass?

m = 2 × KE ÷ v² = (2 × 150) ÷ (10)² = 300 ÷ 100 = 3 kg

Problem 4: Velocity Effect on KE

A 2kg object at 5 m/s speeds up to 10 m/s. What is the change in kinetic energy?

Initial KE = ½ × 2 × (5)² = 1 × 25 = 25 J
Final KE = ½ × 2 × (10)² = 1 × 100 = 100 J
Change = 100 - 25 = 75 Joules

Problem 5: Comparing Kinetic Energies

Which has more KE: a 5kg object at 4 m/s or a 2kg object at 10 m/s?

Object 1: KE = ½ × 5 × (4)² = 2.5 × 16 = 40 J
Object 2: KE = ½ × 2 × (10)² = 1 × 100 = 100 J
Object 2 has more KE (100 J > 40 J)

Problem 6: Work-Energy Theorem

A car accelerates from rest to 20 m/s. If the car has a mass of 1000kg, how much work was done? (Assume constant acceleration)

Work = ΔKE = Final KE - Initial KE
Final KE = ½ × 1000 × (20)² = 500 × 400 = 200,000 J
Initial KE = 0 (at rest)
Work = 200,000 - 0 = 200,000 Joules or 200 kJ
