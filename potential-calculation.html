Problem 1: Basic Gravitational PE

A 2kg object is lifted to a height of 5 meters. Calculate its potential energy. (g = 10 m/s²)

PE = mgh = 2 × 10 × 5 = 100 Joules

Problem 2: Finding Mass

An object has 500 Joules of potential energy at a height of 10m. What is its mass? (g = 10 m/s²)

Mass = PE ÷ (g × h) = 500 ÷ (10 × 10) = 500 ÷ 100 = 5 kg

Problem 3: Finding Height

A 3kg object has 600 Joules of potential energy. What height is it at? (g = 10 m/s²)

Height = PE ÷ (m × g) = 600 ÷ (3 × 10) = 600 ÷ 30 = 20 meters

Problem 4: Elastic PE Calculation

A spring with spring constant k = 200 N/m is compressed 0.3m. What is its elastic PE?

PE = ½kx² = ½ × 200 × (0.3)² = 100 × 0.09 = 9 Joules

Problem 5: Comparing PE at Different Heights

A 10kg object is first at height 2m, then lifted to height 5m. Find the change in PE. (g = 10 m/s²)

PE₁ = 10 × 10 × 2 = 200 J
PE₂ = 10 × 10 × 5 = 500 J
Change in PE = 500 - 200 = 300 Joules

Problem 6: Falling Object Energy Conversion

A 4kg object falls from 6m height and hits the ground. If all PE converts to KE, what is the final KE? (g = 10 m/s²)

Initial PE = mgh = 4 × 10 × 6 = 240 J
Final KE = 240 J (by conservation of energy)
Or using v: KE = ½mv² = ½ × 4 × v² = 240, so v = √120 ≈ 11 m/s
