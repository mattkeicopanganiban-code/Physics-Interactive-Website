4. A compressed spring in a toy

ELASTIC PE - The spring is compressed and stores elastic potential energy

GRAVITATIONAL PE - The apple has height above the ground

6. Wood before it's burned

Categorize by PE comparison:

Which has MORE potential energy?

1. A 5kg object at 2m height OR a 3kg object at 4m height?

5kg at 2m: PE = 5 × 10 × 2 = 100 J
3kg at 4m: PE = 3 × 10 × 4 = 120 J
Answer: The 3kg object at 4m has MORE PE

2. A spring stretched 10cm OR a spring stretched 5cm?

The spring stretched 10cm has MORE PE (PE ∝ x², so doubled stretch = 4x energy)

3. A ball at 10m height at Earth OR at 10m height on the Moon?

The ball at 10m on Earth has MORE PE (g on Earth ≈ 10 m/s²; g on Moon ≈ 1.6 m/s²)

4. An object at sea level OR an object at mountain top (same mass)?

The object at the mountain top has MORE PE (greater height = greater gravitational PE)
